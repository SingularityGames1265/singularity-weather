About: Creating the Void Plane

The Void Plane was originally created to explore the cohesion of the concious and subconcious. While people tend to see these as two completely mirroring things, they can work together quite well. Many entries of the Void Plane were created from the dreams of the creator: Singularity, or Singu for short. The word “void” is usually interpereted as something cold, dark and bleak. But the Void Plane aims to put it into a different context. Many of the regions of it could count as empty spaces, lacking humans or human interference. But these places are meant to be welcoming, all be it unspoken.

It is common for people to feel uneasy when visiting an empty place that is usually filled with human activity. But rarely, it can feel almost comforting. There is the sense of familiarity present, but no constant stimuli screaming for attention. The world of today is incredibly fast paced. The intent of the Void Plane is to allow someone to take a breather, without someone else taking it from them.

There are many aesthetics that inspire the Void Plane, the major one being liminal spaces. Liminal spaces are meant to be used to travel from one location to another. These can be spaces like roads, hallways or stations. In modern media, they are presented as something scary. But this is something the Void Plane is aspiring not to be. Even if a bit strange, the regions are meant to be welcoming, not frightening.

All entries to the Void Plane are written with care. First on paper and eventually added to the Void Plane archive. Some entries also contain imagery, these are made using acrylic paint.
