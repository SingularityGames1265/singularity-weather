Cumulus Clouds

What are cumulus clouds?

Cumulus clouds are commonly known as small clouds that look a bit like cotton balls. They are formed when moist air rises. When the air gets high enough, it becomes cold enough for it to condensate, making them stay in position to form a cloud.

What other forms of cumulus clouds are there?

You have two other kinds of cumulus clouds; Altocumulus and Cirrocumulus. Altocumulus clouds appear higher then their common counterpart.

They tend to be smaller and closer together as well. Cirrocumulus clouds exist at the very top of the troposphere, these are even smaller. It might be difficult to see the difference between alto- and cirrocumulus clouds, but cirrocumulus clouds are very thin, making them have incredibly little shade.
